What happens when you initialize a repository? Why do you need to do it?
	It creates a .git file. A .git file is required to create repository and track changes.

How is the staging area different from the working directory and the repository? What value do you think it offers?
	The staging area is a temporary location between the working directory and the repository.  To seperate and verify your files before you make a commit.

How can you use the staging area to make sure you have one commit per logical change?
	You can verify what you are about to verify before making the commit and adding the commit message.

What are some situations when branches would be helpful in keeping your history organized? How would branches help?
	When you want to work on a seperate version but keep the original as a seperate and editaable file.  You would then have two seperate and editable versions.

How do the diagrams help you visualize the branch structure?
	By showing a visual linear representation.

What is the result of merging two branches together? Why do we represent it in the diagram the way we do?
	It combines all the commits from each branch into one.
